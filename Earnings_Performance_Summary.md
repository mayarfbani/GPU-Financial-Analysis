# Earnings Performance Summary

The first quarter of 2026 has solidified the "AI Infrastructure" era. The latest earnings reports from the semiconductor sector show a clear divergence: companies focused on AI data centers and advanced networking are hitting record highs, while those tied to traditional PC and general-purpose enterprise markets are facing a slower recovery.

Below is the analysis of the latest quarterly results (reported in early 2026).

## 1. NVIDIA (FY2026 Q4 / Full Year)

NVIDIA delivered what analysts called a "mic drop" quarter, proving that the demand for AI training and inference has not yet peaked.

- **Revenue:** $68.1 Billion (Q4), up 73% year-over-year. Full-year revenue hit a record $215.9 Billion.
- **EPS:** $1.76 (GAAP), beating expectations as margins stayed resilient at 75%.
- **Forward Guidance:** Projected Q1 FY2027 revenue of $78.0 Billion, signaling that the ramp-up of the Blackwell architecture is offseting any seasonal declines.

## 2. AMD (Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 Guidance)

AMD is successfully positioning itself as the "only viable alternative" to NVIDIA, with its Instinct MI300 and MI325X accelerators gaining massive traction.

- **Revenue:** $10.27 Billion (Q4), beating consensus of $9.65B.
- **EPS:** $1.53, a significant beat against the $1.32 estimate.
- **Forward Guidance:** Guided Q1 2026 revenue to $9.8 Billion. While this is a 5% sequential drop (due to seasonal gaming/PC weakness), it represents 32% year-over-year growth in the crucial Data Center segment.

## 3. Broadcom (FY2026 Q1)

Broadcom has emerged as the "backbone" of the AI data center, dominating the custom chip (XPU) and high-speed networking markets.

- **Revenue:** $19.31 Billion, up 29% year-over-year. AI-specific revenue grew 106% to $8.4 billion.
- **EPS:** $2.10 (Non-GAAP), up 28% year-over-year.
- **Forward Guidance:** Raised Q2 AI semiconductor guidance to $10.7 Billion (140% growth). Management noted they have secured manufacturing capacity through 2028 to meet demand.

## 4. Marvell Technology (FY2026 Q4)

Marvell's results highlight the transition of the data center from traditional "storage" to "AI-connectivity."

- **Revenue:** $2.22 Billion, up 22% year-over-year. Data Center revenue alone grew 46% for the full year.
- **EPS:** $0.80, beating the $0.79 forecast.
- **Forward Guidance:** Issued very strong guidance for Q1 FY2027 (ending April 2026) of $2.40 Billion, as their custom AI silicon programs for "Hyperscalers" (Google/Amazon) continue to scale.

## 5. Intel (Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 Guidance)

Intel remains in a heavy investment phase, with results showing the financial strain of its "IDM 2.0" foundry build-out.

- **Revenue:** $13.67 Billion (Q4), but guided for a significant drop to $11.7B–$12.7B for Q1 2026.
- **EPS:** Reported a full-year net loss for 2025. Q1 2026 guidance is pinned at $0.00 (breakeven).
- **Forward Guidance:** Management expects Q1 to be the "bottom" for supply constraints, with a meaningful acceleration in the second half of 2026 as the Intel 18A process node enters mass production.
